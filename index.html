About me

I'm a rising 5th year PhD student in Operations Research and Information Engineering at Cornell University. I'm fortunate to be advised by Ziv Scully and working with Alexander Terenin and Peter Frazier. I am broadly interested in sequential decision-making under uncertainty, combining data-driven approaches such as Bayesian optimization, reinforcement learning, and LLM, with theoretical insights from Gittins index theory (e.g., Pandora's box problem, Markovian/Bayesian bandits) and stochastic control. I obtained my MS degree from NYU, under the supervision of Li Jin, focusing on queuing control for network systems. Prior to that, I earned a B.Eng. degree in computer science from Yao Class, Institute of Interdisciplinary Information Sciences at Tsinghua University.
